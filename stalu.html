STA LUCIA HIGH SCHOOL

About Us

Sta. Lucia High School is a public secondary school located in Pasig City, Philippines. It was officially established on June 19, 1994, through Republic Act 7767. The school initially operated with a 24-room building, which was inaugurated on March 10, 1998. It opened for the academic year 1998-1999 with 1,086 students and a faculty of 34 teachers, supported by five non-teaching staff. Over the years, the school expanded significantly due to increasing student enrollment. Additional buildings were constructed with the support of the Pasig City government and the Department of Education. In 2007, a new 1.4-hectare campus was acquired, and a 34-room building was constructed to accommodate more students. The school has continued to develop its facilities to enhance the quality of education it provides. Sta. Lucia High School offers both Junior and Senior High School programs, including various academic tracks under the K-12 curriculum. It has a strong academic reputation, consistently performing well in national assessments and competitions. The school is under the jurisdiction of the Schools Division Office of Pasig City and operates with the motto "LUCIANS, Magaling at Disiplinado" (Lucians, Excellent and Disciplined)​

Academics

Junior High School (Grades 7-10) The junior high school program follows the standard curriculum set by the Department of Education (DepEd) and includes core subjects like Mathematics, Science, English, Filipino, Araling Panlipunan, and Edukasyon sa Pagpapakatao (ESP). The school also provides specialized learning modules for students at risk to ensure that they receive adequate academic support​. Senior High School (Grades 11-12) Sta. Lucia High School offers two main tracks for senior high school students: Academic Track: Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) Accounting, Business, and Management (ABM) Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) Track: Information and Communications Technology (ICT) (Computer Systems Servicing) Home Economics (HE) (Bread and Pastry Production, Cookery, and Food & Beverage Services) The school aims to provide students with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills to prepare them for college or employment.

Admission

For Junior High School, the school follows the Department of Education’s standard curriculum, and students typically need to provide proof of previous grade completion (e.g., report cards) for enrollment. Senior High School students can choose from different academic and technical-vocational strands. The school provides self-learning modules (SLMs) to support education.
